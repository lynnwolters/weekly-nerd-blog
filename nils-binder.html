Design For Flexibility

March 6th, 2024

Nils Binder, a front-end developer at 9elements in Germany, shared insights about his work and the evolution of web development within his team during his presentation.

Nils works within 9elements' web development unit, where he is responsible for developing small to medium-sized websites for various target audiences. He emphasized that while his team operates mainly independently, they sometimes collaborate with other units within the company, such as the cyber security unit, to tackle complex projects.

An important topic Nils covered was the evolution in web layout design. He discussed the transition from traditional methods, where wrapper elements were used to give content a max-width, to a more modern approach. This involves techniques such as efficient item centering and adding padding.

Nils also introduced a newer approach using CSS features like margin-inline with dynamic calculations such as max(1.5rem, ((100% - 75rem)) / 2). This method allows developers to be more flexible with space within a layout, depending on screen size and other variables.

Another key aspect of his presentation was the collaboration between designers and developers. Nils stressed the importance of tools like Figma, where designers can work in a developer mode, converting design units like pixels into units like percentages and rem (root em). He also discussed using fr units to create responsive grids, where column spacing adjusts to the available screen space.

Lastly, Nils encouraged regular communication with designers to ensure both teams understand how the technical implementation works. This ensures the final product is both aesthetically pleasing and functional, which is essential for successful collaboration between design and development at 9elements.

In summary, Nils Binder's talk provided profound insights into the daily practices of a front-end developer in an innovative company, highlighting technical details and collaboration strategies to deliver effective and modern web experiences.
